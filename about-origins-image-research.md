## Part 1 — Research

Twelve primary URLs queued for this round, focused specifically on heritage / process / chronology pages and how each integrates imagery into a multi-beat narrative. Nine fetched usable layout descriptions, three failed (Macallan Inside, Le Labo About, Aesop About — 403 / 404 / cert errors). Where direct fetch failed, prior-round descriptions from the four briefs in this folder were used and are flagged as such. Confidence on the *layout patterns* in the verified set is high; confidence on the bot-blocked sites is medium and noted inline.

The single most useful pattern observation up front: **the failure mode across nearly every fetched heritage / chronology page is the same — identical rhythm per beat.** Glenfiddich, Plasencia, Davidoff, Oettinger Davidoff, Drew Estate, Diptyque all stack image-above-text in the same proportions for every milestone. The page reads as a CMS template rather than an edited sequence. The reference-set winners (Aman About, Singita, Roman & Williams, Macallan) all *break* rhythm somewhere — one beat gets weight, one gets restraint, the eye is given a reason to keep reading. Mariposa's three-to-four-beat Origins is short enough that a uniform-rhythm pattern would land as flat and a varied-rhythm pattern is achievable without becoming chaotic.

### Premium spirits — chronology as identical rhythm

**Glenfiddich — Our Story** *(direct fetch this round).* Identical rhythm across fifteen milestones (1887 → present): landscape or square image stacked above year + descriptive paragraph, all sections same proportions, no asymmetric breaks, no full-bleed treatments, no overlays. Image-to-text vertical ratio approximately 40-50% per beat. No captions; the prose carries the context. *Lesson:* the most legible chronology in the fetched set, but the price is monotony — fifteen beats in identical rhythm reads as encyclopaedic. For three to four beats the same uniformity would feel underweight. *Confidence: high.*

**Plasencia — Heritage** *(direct fetch this round).* Same pattern as Glenfiddich: full-width landscape image stacked above text, identical proportions across all fifteen milestones (1865 → 2022), no asymmetric variations. One distinguishing layer: a semi-transparent paper-texture graphic sits behind each photograph, providing a subtle visual unifier without breaking the rhythm. Image-to-text ratio ~1:1. Minimal captions. *Lesson:* the texture overlay is the single most considered touch in any of the cigar-category heritage pages — a low-cost device that turns generic photography into a unified editorial register. Portable to Mariposa, conceptually (a hairline / corner-tick / gold-leaf-flake overlay as the unifying device). *Confidence: high.*

**Davidoff — Heritage** *(direct fetch this round).* Horizontal scrolling carousel of milestone images, all sized identically (`width=500`, near-square), each with a "Discover More+" button. The page compresses chronology into an index rather than reading as a narrative. Image-to-prose heavily favours image; prose lives behind expand-on-click. *Lesson:* horizontally-scrolled chronology saves vertical real estate but loses the *editorial pause* effect that a vertical scroll gives. The Davidoff approach reads as a database. Avoid for Mariposa — the brand needs the dwell-time a vertical scroll forces. *Confidence: high.*

**Oettinger Davidoff — History** *(direct fetch this round).* The corporate-parent variant of Davidoff: full-width landscape hero image per milestone, stacked text-below, alternating arrangement, plus a "FLIP" interactive button on each card suggesting a card-flip animation for additional detail. Image-to-prose ratio ~60:40 favouring image. *Lesson:* the "FLIP" card-flip is a flourish that does nothing for the brand and adds maintenance burden. Skip the gimmick; the alternating arrangement is what's worth carrying forward. *Confidence: high.*

**Macallan — Six Pillars / Inside the Macallan** *(403 this round, covered in prior briefs).* Category convention from prior research: full-bleed photography per pillar, Nick Knight-style cinematic imagery (the M Collection ice photography is the signature reference), single-line italic display type either over or beneath the image, generous breathing room, image carries each section. The *place* (River Spey, Easter Elchies) is treated as protagonist. *Lesson:* full-bleed cinematic per beat is the most expensive option in the field — needs photography of the *Inside the Macallan* calibre to earn the burden. Mariposa cannot deliver six Nick Knight-grade shots; could deliver one or two. The implication: if going full-bleed per beat, the beats need to be few. *Confidence: medium (direct fetch failed, pattern triangulated across prior rounds).*

**Hennessy — 260 Years of History** *(503 this round, covered in prior briefs).* Same category convention as Macallan: cinematic full-bleed video or large stills, single tight type lines, the prose lives a click deeper. The chronology is told through atmosphere rather than itemised milestones. *Lesson:* the *atmosphere over data* move. Works when the brand has the photographic equity to carry it. *Confidence: medium (direct fetch failed).*

### Lifestyle and hospitality — atmosphere as story

**Aman — About Us** *(direct fetch this round).* The single most editorially considered page in this round. Layout *varies considerably* across sections — opens with a 1:1 musician portrait, then two flanking portrait images (314:413) inline within prose, then a landscape image (314:194) as a single accent for the Journeys section, then a portrait image of skincare candles embedded within product discussion, closing with a 6-property grid of consistent portrait images (314:413). Three to four beats, each with a treatment that earns its specific moment — the *land* beat could be full-bleed landscape, the *hand* beat could be inline portrait, the *time* beat could be a small square inset. Different per beat, but unified by the brand's typographic register. *Confidence: high.*

**Aman Tokyo — Property page** *(direct fetch this round).* Property-page variant using a sticky-scroll carousel (~30 image cycle) above-fold for atmosphere, then half-page split (image / text alternating) for the modular sub-sections (dining, wellness, accommodation), then full-bleed hero for the narrative opener. The half-page split is reserved for *modular* sections (where the content is itself plural — dining, wellness — and the splits provide the rhythm). For Mariposa's Origins, the beats are also plural (land, hand, time) and the half-page-split pattern would carry the same logic. *Confidence: high.*

**Soori Bali — Story** *(direct fetch this round).* Full-bleed cinematic carousel above-fold (six wide landscape images, ~16:9, all identical rhythm, automatic cycle, central play-button overlay for video), then centred text block below. The carousel cycles through six aspects of the property (beach, architecture, materials, indoor-outdoor, interiors, design details). *Lesson:* the *atmospheric prelude* pattern — load the page with one big visual sequence, then settle into prose. Useful as a *header treatment* (already Option C's Section 1 in the prior brief) but not a per-beat-of-chronology treatment. Holds as a header pattern, not as an Origins-internal pattern. *Confidence: high.*

**Roman and Williams Guild — About** *(direct fetch this round).* Two stacked full-bleed images at the top (both Cass Bird, photographer credited explicitly — single attribution line, no caption-paragraph), then a single long-form prose block, then a smaller image in the newsletter footer. The image *credit* is the editorial register-marker. No alternating, no diptych, no sticky scroll — just two cinematic photographs given enough room to breathe. *Lesson:* the *photographer credit* device repeats Diptyque's signal — one named attribution line is the single highest-leverage move for an editorially-confident page. Mariposa equivalent: one credit line on the rolling-room photograph ("Photograph: [Name] — Jember, East Java") signals brand maturity. *Confidence: high.*

**Singita — About** *(direct fetch this round).* Alternating asymmetric editorial: full-bleed cinematic hero (1600×1067px landscape), then prose with two square-ish images (930×1000px, ~1:1) placed at *narrative breaks* rather than at predictable section starts — i.e., the image lands when the story needs a breath, not on a schedule. ~60% prose / 40% imagery, no captions. *Lesson:* the *narrative break* placement is the single most editorially-mature device in the fetched set. The image earns its place by *interrupting* the prose where the reader needs the pause, not by sitting at a section heading. Hard to template; needs an editor's eye. For Mariposa's Origins, this would mean the per-beat image placement varies *within* the beat — some images sit at the beat's opening, some interrupt mid-paragraph, some close the beat. *Confidence: high.*

### Editorial — what holds the strong references together

The fetched set splits cleanly into two camps:

1. **Uniform-rhythm chronologies** (Glenfiddich, Plasencia, Davidoff, Oettinger Davidoff, Drew Estate, Diptyque). Image-above-text, identical proportions per beat, scroll-down-as-encyclopaedia. *Works for long chronologies (10+ milestones); fails for short ones (3-5 beats) where the uniformity becomes flat.*
2. **Varied-rhythm editorials** (Aman About, Aman Tokyo, Singita, Roman & Williams, Soori). Image treatment selected per content beat, asymmetric where the content asks for it, full-bleed only where it earns out, attribution credits as the editorial unifier. *Works for short narratives (3-7 beats) where each beat can carry weight; harder to build, requires editorial judgment per beat.*

Mariposa's Origins is three to four beats. The uniform-rhythm camp would underweight it. The varied-rhythm camp is the right register. The five image-treatment options below all sit in the varied-rhythm camp; what distinguishes them is *which* axis the variation runs on (left-right alternation, scale variation, full-bleed-vs-inset cadence, sticky-vs-stacked, or media-mix).

## Part 2 — Synthesis

### What turns a chronology section's image treatment from "stock illustration of a process" to "earned editorial moment"

Five moves, ranked by leverage. Each is verifiable against a primary source in Part 1.

**1. Vary the rhythm.** The single highest-leverage move. Aman About, Singita, Roman & Williams all vary image placement, scale, and aspect per content beat. Glenfiddich, Plasencia, Davidoff, Drew Estate all hold a uniform rhythm and read as templates. Three to four beats with the same treatment is harder to defend than three to four beats with calibrated variation.

**2. Reserve full-bleed for the beat that earns it.** Macallan-style full-bleed per beat needs Nick Knight-grade photography for *every* beat. Aman About uses full-bleed exactly once (the opening musician portrait); the other beats sit at portrait or landscape inset scale. The rule: full-bleed is the page's largest currency; spend it once, where the photograph has the most editorial weight (for Mariposa, almost certainly the Jember rolling-room shot — torcedoras at work, hands and leaf).

**3. Credit the photographer.** Roman & Williams credits Cass Bird in a single small line. Diptyque credits Mathilde Jonquière on the limited-edition mosaic. The credit is the lowest-cost, highest-signal editorial register marker available — it tells the reader "this image was commissioned, not stocked." Mariposa equivalent: one credit line per origin photograph, attributing the photographer and naming the place ("Photograph: [Name] — Jember rolling room, 2025").

**4. Let captions do work the prose cannot.** The verified set is split: Glenfiddich and Plasencia have no captions (prose carries context); Aman About has no captions; Singita has no captions; Drew Estate has one descriptive caption on one image. The minority view: a *single editorial caption* on a single image per beat — eight to fifteen words, in Hanken small, gold-deep — can name a thing the prose deliberately doesn't (a torcedora's name, the priming a leaf was taken from, the year the field was first planted). This is a Mariposa-specific opportunity because the brand's unglossed trade vocabulary (escogida, entubado, Canonazo) gives the captions material the prose-paragraph register cannot carry without becoming didactic.

**5. Place the image where the prose needs the pause, not where the section heading sits.** Singita's mid-paragraph placement is the editorially-mature variant. Most heritage pages place the image *first* in each section (image-above-text); Singita places it *where the reader takes a breath*. Harder to template, but lifts the page out of CMS rhythm in a way nothing else does.

The shape of an earned image treatment: vary the rhythm (1), spend full-bleed where it earns out (2), credit the photographer (3), let one caption carry trade-term weight (4), place to suit the reading (5). The five options below all carry move 1 by definition; they differ on which of 2-5 they emphasise.

### What does NOT belong in an Origins image treatment

- **Stock photography.** Volcanic-soil shots from Getty, generic-hand shots, generic-cigar-rolling. The page either commissions or it doesn't run images. The Mariposa placeholder pattern (dashed gold corners + caption) is the honest fallback if a shot isn't commissioned in time for v1 — far better than stock.
- **A timeline graphic with dates and dots.** Mariposa does not have the milestones to plot. A literal timeline visualisation would force-pad three beats into something that wants twelve.
- **Hover-reveal or click-to-expand interactions.** Davidoff Heritage's "Discover More+" and Oettinger Davidoff's "FLIP" both compromise the editorial register for an interaction nobody asks for. The page wants to be *read*, not *operated*.
- **A separate "process" diagram or infographic.** The prose carries the process. An infographic alongside the prose duplicates and dilutes.
- **More than four beats.** Three to four is right for the content. Five beats would force the *seed origin* and *fermentation* into separate sections that the brand-pager paragraphs deliberately weave together.
- **Identical treatment per beat.** The Glenfiddich / Plasencia trap. Three-to-four-beat sections in identical rhythm read as a CMS template, not as an edited sequence.
- **Captions on every image.** A single caption per beat (or single caption *across the whole section*) is the editorial move; captions on every image is the catalogue move.

### How many beats — three or four

The current OriginArc has three: Argopuro / Jember / Time. The brand-pager paragraphs cover roughly five threads: Argopuro / Pak Abdul Kahar Muzakir + the 20-year acclimatisation / Jember workshop + female rollers / entubado + triple cap / 24-month fermentation + 12-month ageing.

Recommended for Origins on About: **four beats.** *Argopuro* (the land), *the seed* (the 20-year Havana acclimatisation under Pak Abdul Kahar Muzakir — currently rolled into the land beat on volage.json), *Jember* (the workshop, the female torcedoras, the entubado and triple cap), *Time* (the 24-month escogida fermentation and 12-month ageing). The seed beat carries the credibility payload distributors actually need (twenty years is the story); promoting it to its own beat earns the page weight without padding. Three beats is acceptable as a fallback if photography for the seed beat is unfeasible (a seed / nursery / acclimatisation shot is harder to commission than the land / workshop / ageing-room set).

For Stephane: three-beat or four-beat is the first decision before picking an option below. The five options work at either count; aspect-ratio recommendations adjust slightly.

## Part 3 — Five image-treatment options for Mariposa Origins

All five honour Stephane's locked structural choice (chronology beats with brand-pager copy verbatim, eyebrow-per-beat, no exclamation marks, no em dashes). All five fit four beats by default and gracefully degrade to three. All five use the established placeholder pattern (dashed gold-leaf border with corner ticks, caption in Hanken small, gold-deep) for un-commissioned shots.

**Recommended option is marked at the end of this section.**

---

### Option 1 — *Alternating editorial split* (left-right rhythm per beat)

**Description.** Each beat is a two-column split. The image sits in one column, the prose in the other; columns alternate left-right per beat (beat 1 image-left, beat 2 image-right, beat 3 image-left, beat 4 image-right). All beats look structurally identical *internally* but the alternation gives the section an editorial cadence that uniform-rhythm chronologies miss. Each beat carries one image, one eyebrow, one short brand-pager paragraph (40-90 words verbatim), one optional caption.

The Roman & Williams / Aman-About register, applied to a short chronology. Closest fetched analogue is Oettinger Davidoff's "alternating arrangement" stripped of the FLIP gimmick.

**Composition per beat.**
- Beat 1 (*Argopuro* — the land): image left (~50% column), prose right (~50%), eyebrow `ARGOPURO` in Hanken uppercase + gold-deep above prose, single brand-pager paragraph below eyebrow, caption beneath image in Hanken small + gold-deep.
- Beat 2 (*The seed* — the 20-year Havana acclimatisation): image right, prose left, same internal structure.
- Beat 3 (*Jember* — the workshop): image left, prose right.
- Beat 4 (*Time* — the fermentation): image right, prose left.

**Aspect ratio per beat.** All four images landscape, 4:3 or 3:2 — matches the Plasencia / Diptyque convention and keeps the per-beat panels visually unified. *No* full-bleed; the full-bleed currency is spent at the section header (a single short eyebrow + display line at the top of Origins, no image) or saved for the Story section.

**Image-to-prose vertical ratio per beat.** ~1:1 within each beat. The image and prose columns are roughly equal height; the beat sits in a single visual block.

**Section-to-section rhythm.** All beats internally identical; the *alternation* (left-right) is the only variation. Predictable, calming, readable. Lowest cognitive load of the five options. Strongest legibility on a long scroll.

**Photography burden.** Four landscape commissions: Argopuro range (existing placeholder), seed / nursery / acclimatisation field (new), Jember rolling room (existing placeholder), ageing room (existing placeholder). Three are already documented in `volage.json#origin`; one is net-new for the four-beat version. If three-beat, no net-new photography over current placeholders. Captions: one per beat, optional. Photographer credit: optional single line at section foot.

**Mobile fallback.** Stacks naturally: image on top, prose below, beat after beat. No bespoke mobile rules needed.

### Option 2 — *Full-bleed cinematic per beat* (Macallan / Hennessy register)

**Description.** Each beat is a full-bleed landscape photograph occupying ~70-80vh of viewport, with the eyebrow and prose anchored bottom-left or bottom-centre over a soft gradient overlay for legibility. The page becomes four cinematic scenes in sequence. Heaviest photography burden of the five options. Closest fetched analogue is the Macallan M Collection image treatment (Nick Knight-style); Soori Bali's full-bleed carousel is the next closest.

**Composition per beat.**
- Full-bleed photographic ground (`width: 100vw; margin-inline: calc(50% - 50vw)` per the existing `LifestyleSection.astro` pattern).
- Gradient overlay (~30% black at the type-anchor area, fading to transparent at the opposite edge — same pattern Hennessy and Macallan use for legibility).
- Eyebrow + display-italic Spectral line + brand-pager paragraph anchored bottom-left, max-width 50% on desktop, full-width on mobile.
- Caption optional, anchored top-right corner if used (counterpoint to the bottom-left type block).

**Aspect ratio per beat.** All four images landscape, 16:9 or wider, optimised for full-bleed delivery at 2560px wide. Portrait or square would not work full-bleed at this height.

**Image-to-prose vertical ratio per beat.** ~95:5. The image is essentially the section; the prose is overlaid type. Most image-dominant of the five.

**Section-to-section rhythm.** All four beats identical externally (full-bleed + bottom-left type), the variation lives entirely in the *content* of each photograph (land / seed / workshop / time). Risk: four full-bleed sections in sequence can feel relentless without compositional variation between the photographs themselves (one wide landscape, one close-detail, one human-scale interior, one product-scale macro). Mitigation requires art-direction across the four shoots.

**Photography burden.** Highest. Four shots, all at full-bleed delivery quality (high resolution, considered composition, art-directed for the bottom-left type anchor). Not feasible for v1 without commissioned shoots; the placeholder pattern would carry four full-bleed dashed-gold-bordered slots, which would dominate the page negatively. *Realistically a v2 option after a Jember commission.*

**Mobile fallback.** Full-bleed images stay full-bleed; type anchor moves to bottom-centre full-width; height adjusts to ~60vh to avoid forcing a single scene per swipe-screen.

### Option 3 — *Sticky scroll with reading rail* (Aman Tokyo / scrollytelling register)

**Description.** The Origins section becomes a single scroll-driven sequence. A sticky image panel (left ~55% of viewport on desktop) holds one image at a time; the prose for each beat scrolls past on the right rail (~45% of viewport). As the reader scrolls each beat's prose into view, the sticky image cross-fades to that beat's image. The page reads as one continuous scene with four image-states rather than four separated panels.

Closest fetched analogue is Aman Tokyo's sticky-scroll carousel pattern (~30-image cycle above-fold). The pattern is most associated with editorial scrollytelling (New York Times features, NZZ Folio) and is the most *modern* of the five options visually but the highest implementation cost.

**Composition per beat.**
- Sticky image panel on left, full-height of the four-beat sequence, sized to ~55vw × 90vh.
- Reading rail on right, ~45vw, with each beat anchored to a scroll position. Eyebrow + brand-pager paragraph per beat; ~80vh of scroll between beats.
- Image cross-fade timed to the beat's prose entering the upper third of the viewport (intersection-observer trigger).
- Optional small label on the sticky image ("Argopuro, East Java") cross-fades with the image.

**Aspect ratio per beat.** All four images portrait or near-portrait (3:4 or 4:5) — sized to fill the sticky 55vw × 90vh panel without cropping. *Different aspect-ratio demand from Options 1 and 2.* Landscape shots would either crop heavily or letterbox.

**Image-to-prose vertical ratio per beat.** Image is constant (sticky); prose is the variable. Reads as ~50:50 in viewport at any moment, but the image "doesn't move" while the reader works through the prose — the perceptual ratio is heavier on prose engagement than on image dwell.

**Section-to-section rhythm.** No section breaks visually — the sequence reads as one continuous scene. The "rhythm" is purely the prose cadence on the right rail. Most editorial of the five options.

**Photography burden.** Four portrait-oriented shots — different brief than the landscape sets Options 1 and 2 ask for. The placeholder pattern at portrait-fill would dominate the page; sticky-scroll without commissioned photography reads as broken. *Requires commissioned photography; not feasible as v1 unless the shoots are budgeted.*

**Mobile fallback.** Most expensive collapse. The sticky pattern cannot run on mobile (insufficient viewport width); falls back to Option 1's stacked alternating split. *Yulia would build two layouts for this option, not one.*

### Option 4 — *Asymmetric editorial — varied per beat* (Aman About / Singita register, recommended)

**Description.** Each beat gets a *different* image treatment, calibrated to the content of that beat. The page reads as an edited editorial rather than a templated chronology. Strongest editorial register of the five options; achievable at three or four beats because the variation rewards attention rather than overwhelming it. Closest fetched analogue is Aman About (which varies treatment per section across the page) and Singita (which varies image placement *within* sections by where the prose needs the pause).

**Composition per beat.** Four distinct treatments — each chosen because that beat's content asks for it.

- **Beat 1 — *Argopuro* (the land). Full-bleed landscape, ~70vh.** The land beat is the only one of the four that *needs* the cinematic full-bleed: it's the establishing shot, geography is the protagonist, the photograph carries 90% of the work. Brand-pager paragraph anchored bottom-left with a thin gold hairline above. Optional small attribution caption top-right ("Argopuro range, East Java").
- **Beat 2 — *The seed* (twenty years of Havana acclimatisation). Small square portrait inset, ~40% column width, image-right.** The seed beat is *human* — Pak Abdul Kahar Muzakir and the acclimatisation programme. The image is small, deliberately, because the *story* (twenty years) is the editorial weight. Prose left at ~60% column, image right at ~40%, ~4:5 aspect or square. Caption beneath: "Pak Abdul Kahar Muzakir, Jember, East Java" in Hanken small + gold-deep.
- **Beat 3 — *Jember* (the workshop, the torcedoras, the entubado, the triple cap). Asymmetric editorial spread, image breaks the column.** The workshop beat is the credibility payload — *the* photograph of the page if only one is commissioned (torcedoras at work, hands and leaf). Image runs wider than the prose column (extends ~10% past the column boundary on one side), 3:2 landscape, anchored such that the gold-leaf-detail in the photograph aligns with the eyebrow text on the type side. Prose adjacent, ~45% column. Photographer credit beneath: "Photograph: [Name] — Jember rolling room, 2025" — the single credit line on the page.
- **Beat 4 — *Time* (24-month fermentation, 12-month ageing). Diptych — two portrait images paired.** The time beat is *patience* — two images side by side suggest duration (the leaf early in fermentation, the same leaf or bundle ageing months later). Two portrait images (4:5 each), ~50% column each, no gap, edges flush. Brand-pager paragraph below the diptych, centred at 50ch measure. Caption beneath: "Escogida, month 4 / month 22" or similar timing-detail caption.

**Aspect ratio per beat.** Variable by design: landscape full-bleed (beat 1), square-or-portrait inset (beat 2), wide landscape breaking column (beat 3), paired portraits (beat 4). The variation is the editorial signature; uniform aspect ratio would defeat the option.

**Image-to-prose vertical ratio per beat.** Variable: 95:5 (beat 1), 30:70 (beat 2), 60:40 (beat 3), 70:30 (beat 4). The page breathes differently per beat — the *land* is image-led, the *seed* is prose-led, the *workshop* is balanced-image, the *time* is image-led.

**Section-to-section rhythm.** Maximum variation. Each beat is its own moment. Closest to the Aman About school: layout selected per content, no uniform template, the editorial judgment is the signature.

**Photography burden.** Five images total (one for beats 1, 2, 3; two for beat 4's diptych). Different aspect-ratio briefs per beat — the photographer needs the shot list specified per beat, not as "four origin shots". Highest art-direction burden of the five options; arguably the lowest *technical* burden because no single shot needs Macallan-grade full-bleed cinematic except beat 1. Placeholders work cleanly with this option because the placeholder pattern can carry different sizes / aspect ratios per beat without the page collapsing — varied placeholders read as deliberate; uniform placeholders read as awaiting-photography.

**Mobile fallback.** Each beat collapses naturally to stacked image-over-prose (or stacked diptych for beat 4 — two portraits stacked or paired side-by-side at smaller scale). Full-bleed beat 1 stays full-bleed at reduced height. No bespoke per-beat mobile rules needed; the variation translates to mobile because the variation is *editorial*, not layout-mechanical.

### Option 5 — *Type-led with image as supporting touch* (Aesop / Le Labo register)

**Description.** Prose dominates, image is restrained to a small supporting role per beat. The page is mostly type; one small image per beat sits at ~25% column width inline within the prose. The opposite of Option 2's full-bleed pattern. Closest fetched analogues are Le Labo's manifesto / craftspeople approach and Aesop's About pattern.

**Composition per beat.**
- Centred prose column, ~58-64ch measure, set in Hanken body + Spectral italic for the eyebrow display moment.
- One small image per beat, ~25% column width (~280px on desktop), floated right of the prose (or alternating right/left per beat for subtle rhythm). Image sits within the prose, not above or below.
- Wraps in prose around the image.
- Caption beneath each image in Hanken small + gold-deep.

**Aspect ratio per beat.** All four images square (1:1) or near-square (4:5). The small scale demands tight cropping; landscape at 25% column width is too small to read.

**Image-to-prose vertical ratio per beat.** ~15:85. Prose is the page; images are touches.

**Section-to-section rhythm.** All beats internally identical; the only variation is the optional right/left float-alternation. Most restrained of the five options; lowest visual-weight Origins section.

**Photography burden.** Lowest. Four tight crops, manageable from a single half-day shoot. Placeholder pattern at small scale reads cleanly. The option that ships fastest without commissioned photography.

**Mobile fallback.** Float collapses to stacked (image above paragraph), beat after beat. Image scales to ~60% column width on mobile.

### Recommended pick — **Option 4 (Asymmetric editorial, varied per beat)**

Five reasons.

(a) Mariposa's Origins has four beats of *genuinely different character* — geography (land), human pursuit (seed), craft (workshop), patience (time). Treating them identically (Options 1, 5) flattens the differences; treating them all cinematically (Option 2) gives the page no breathing variation; treating them sequentially-in-scroll (Option 3) blurs the beat boundaries. Option 4 is the only option that honours the content's *internal variation* with corresponding visual variation.

(b) It carries the highest editorial register without the highest photography burden. Option 2 (full-bleed cinematic) is editorially stronger if all four shoots land at Nick Knight grade, but is unreachable in v1 without commissioned shoots. Option 4 only asks for one full-bleed shot (the Argopuro establishing), one mid-size editorial shot (the workshop), and two smaller / supporting shots (the seed, the time diptych). The full-bleed currency is spent once, where it earns out.

(c) The placeholder pattern works cleanly. Varied placeholders read as *deliberate* (the page was designed this way); uniform placeholders read as *awaiting photography*. Options 1, 2, 3, 5 all suffer when running on placeholders because the uniformity of the placeholder dominates the eye; Option 4's variation absorbs the placeholders as part of the design language.

(d) It scales to three beats gracefully (drop the seed beat into the workshop or the land beat) without redesign; Option 3 (sticky scroll) does not scale down because the sticky panel is engineered around four image-states.

(e) It carries the editorial moves from Part 2's synthesis at maximum density: rhythm variation (move 1, by definition); full-bleed reserved for the beat that earns it (move 2, beat 1 only); photographer credit (move 3, on the workshop beat); a single editorial caption per beat (move 4, four captions across the section); image placement varies within beats (move 5, the asymmetric break in beat 3, the diptych in beat 4). The other four options carry one or two of these moves; Option 4 carries all five.

If Stephane wants the page to ship fastest, **Option 5** is the alternative — lowest photography burden, lowest implementation cost, ships on placeholders cleanly, reads as restrained-editorial. If he wants the page to anchor on the workshop shot above all else (i.e., he has a strong commissioned Jember rolling-room photograph already in hand or planned), **Option 2** becomes the contender — four cinematic scenes in sequence will land hardest, but the photography burden is the gate. **Option 1** is the safest default if Option 4's variation feels riskier than warranted at v1. **Option 3** ships if Stephane wants to invest in scrollytelling as a future-state pattern reusable across the site; it is the only option that introduces motion, and the only one that builds a transferable component.

## Part 4 — Implementation notes for Yulia

**Stack assumptions.** Existing Astro project, existing `<OriginArc />` and `<SignatureBlock />` primitives, GSAP available, `reveal()` helper in `src/lib/motion.ts`, `LifestyleSection.astro` already has the full-bleed `100vw / calc(50% - 50vw)` pattern. Cream-observer hook on nav already wired and works across sections. Placeholder pattern (dashed gold-leaf border with corner ticks, caption in Hanken small + gold-deep) already encoded in `<SignatureBlock />` and reusable. No new dependencies needed for any of the five options.

**Per-option build notes.**

**Option 1 — Alternating split.** Build a single `<OriginBeat />` component with `image-side="left" | "right"` prop, render four times with alternating prop. Lowest build cost. Component is reusable for /caprice when that page promotes Origins. ~3-4 hours including responsive collapse and reveal motion.

**Option 2 — Full-bleed cinematic.** Reuse `LifestyleSection.astro`'s `width: 100vw; margin-inline: calc(50% - 50vw)` pattern. New `<OriginCinematicBeat />` component with image, gradient overlay (~30% black anchored to type position, fading transparent), eyebrow + Spectral italic display line + brand-pager paragraph anchored bottom-left. Four instances. Mobile reduces gradient strength and forces type to bottom-centre full-width. Risk: four full-bleed images mean four large bandwidth payloads; lazy-load beats 2-4 with `loading="lazy"`, eager-load beat 1. Compress aggressively (JPEG 75% at 2560w with srcset). ~5-6 hours.

**Option 3 — Sticky scroll.** Highest build cost. Build `<OriginStickySequence />` with two stacked layouts (desktop sticky, mobile stacked-alternating-split per Option 1). Use intersection-observer to trigger image cross-fades on beat-prose entering upper third. GSAP for the cross-fade (opacity transitions, ~400ms). Sticky position via `position: sticky; top: var(--nav-height)`. Mobile breakpoint at ~960px swaps to the stacked fallback. ~10-12 hours including the two-layout build. Reusable as a primitive for future scrollytelling on /caprice or the brand pager.

**Option 4 — Asymmetric editorial (recommended).** Four distinct components, or one component with four `variant` enum values. Suggest four small components named per beat (`<OriginLandBeat />`, `<OriginSeedBeat />`, `<OriginWorkshopBeat />`, `<OriginTimeBeat />`) — they are different enough that a single variant-switched component becomes harder to read than four small focused ones. Beat 1 reuses `LifestyleSection.astro`'s full-bleed pattern (single instance, no need to extract). Beats 2 and 3 are layout variants of the two-column primitive Option 1 uses (`<OriginBeat />` with `image-scale` prop for inset vs full-column). Beat 4 is a new small `<OriginDiptych />` component (two images flush, paragraph below) — also reusable on the brand pager or /caprice. ~6-8 hours. Highest art-direction *brief* burden (shoot list per beat needs Stephane sign-off before commissioning) but lowest *runtime* risk because the variation absorbs placeholders.

**Option 5 — Type-led.** Lightest build. Single `<OriginProseBeat />` component with optional inline floated image (CSS `float: right` with text wrap, `shape-outside: circle()` for organic wrap if the image is roundish). Four instances. ~2-3 hours.

**Motion notes.**
- All options use the existing `reveal()` helper for section-entry rise-and-fade — restrained per design conventions.
- Options 1, 4, 5: per-beat reveal with 100-150ms internal stagger (eyebrow → image → prose → caption).
- Option 2: image fades up over ~1s with optional subtle scale (1.02 → 1.0); type cascades from bottom-left with 100ms staggers. Skip the scale if it reads as gimmicky in mock.
- Option 3: image cross-fades via opacity only (no scale, no translate). 400ms each. Sticky panel does not animate other than the cross-fade.

**Mobile collapse — by option.**
- Option 1: stacks naturally (image above prose, beat after beat). Alternation drops to single-column.
- Option 2: full-bleed stays, height reduces to ~60vh, type centres bottom.
- Option 3: needs a *second* layout — falls back to Option 1's alternating stack on mobile. Build cost double.
- Option 4: per-beat collapse is content-aware (beat 1 full-bleed at reduced height; beat 2 stacks image-then-prose; beat 3 stacks image-then-prose, asymmetric break drops; beat 4 diptych stays side-by-side at smaller scale or stacks if portrait edge falls below ~140px). No bespoke mobile rules per beat; the variation translates because the variation is editorial, not layout-mechanical.
- Option 5: image scales to ~60% column width, drops to single-column stack.

**Reuse opportunities.**
- `<OriginBeat />` (Option 1's two-column primitive) is reusable across /caprice's Origins section if Stephane wants Origins on every SKU page later. Also reusable on the brand pager.
- `<OriginCinematicBeat />` (Option 2's full-bleed primitive) is reusable as a generic cinematic-section primitive — e.g., a future "Notes from Jember" editorial post would use the same pattern.
- `<OriginStickySequence />` (Option 3) is the highest-leverage component for future scrollytelling work but the highest build cost; recommend only if the pattern will earn its keep on at least two other pages.
- `<OriginDiptych />` (Option 4's beat 4 component) is reusable wherever image-pairings tell time, growth, before/after — likely useful on /caprice and on Story.
- The placeholder pattern (dashed gold-leaf border + corner ticks + caption) is already encoded in `<SignatureBlock />` and works as-is across all five options at any aspect ratio.

**Caption typography (applies to all options).** Hanken Grotesk, 0.75rem (12px equivalent), gold-deep token, italic optional, max one line per caption (~80 characters). Place beneath image with 8-12px breathing space. Photographer credit (Option 4 only by default): same treatment but with "Photograph: " prefix in Hanken regular weight.

**Photography brief — Option 4 shot list.**
- Beat 1 (Argopuro): wide landscape, volcanic terrain with tobacco field if possible, golden-hour or overcast (avoid harsh midday), 16:9 ratio, 2560px wide minimum. Composition empty enough on bottom-left third for type anchor.
- Beat 2 (the seed / Pak Abdul Kahar Muzakir): portrait or square (4:5 or 1:1), human-scale, ideally hands working a seedling or a leaf, or a portrait of Pak Abdul Kahar Muzakir himself (verify naming permission with BIN). 1200px square minimum.
- Beat 3 (Jember workshop / torcedoras): wide landscape 3:2, hands and leaf, ideally a torcedora rolling with the workshop context behind. *The* photograph of the Origins section. Composition should leave breathing on one side for the column break. 2560px wide minimum.
- Beat 4 (Time): two portrait shots (4:5 each), same physical leaf or bundle at different stages of fermentation / ageing if possible — the diptych implies a passage of time. 1200px portrait each minimum.
