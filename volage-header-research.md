# Volage header — research brief and four header proposals

**Author.** Pax.
**Date.** 2026-05-24.
**Status.** First-pass deliverable for Stephane. Yulia is the intended builder.
**Brief.** The Volage page (`/volage`) currently opens with a 50/50 split header — a 1:1 gold-framed cigar image on the left, a four-line type ladder on the right (edition eyebrow, display name, vitola, italic tagline). The information stays the same. Stephane wants four alternative ways to *present* this information visually. Same calibre as the prior `story-section-research.md` and `product-image-research.md` briefs and uses the same format.

The page below this header is now sequenced: signature (gold leaf) → cigar narrative + spec strips → smoke arc → packaging gallery → cross-link to Caprice → footer. Whatever the header does, it has to land an editorial pause heavy enough to earn the run of editorial moments below.

## Part 1 — Research

Twenty primary URLs queued; thirteen returned usable visual / structural descriptions, seven were 403 / 404 / SSL-failed / timed out (Macallan, Hermès H24, Vacheron, Aman accommodation page, IWC, Cohiba Black, Audemars Piguet specific reference). Where direct fetch failed I leant on the prior two rounds of research (covered in the briefs in this folder) and on the press / commentary sources cited. Confidence on the *direction* the category takes is high; confidence on specific compositional details of bot-blocked sites is medium per site and flagged inline.

The single most useful observation up front, so the rest reads as evidence: **single-SKU pages at this register almost never lead with a split type/image card.** The split card is a DTC reflex. Premium single-SKU pages lead with one of four moves — full-bleed photographic room, type-led editorial pause, split with one column doing 80% of the work, or a *deferred* hero where the brand statement comes first and the SKU detail second. The current Volage header is a textbook DTC split. The four options below pull from the four premium moves.

### Editorial / design theory — what holds the four together

The pattern across every fetched source above is consistent: a single-SKU hero at this register has one protagonist. The protagonist is either an object, a line, a name, or an atmosphere. The hero that fails is the one that tries to give equal weight to four elements (edition / name / vitola / tagline) on a 50/50 split, which is what the current Volage header does. Editorial design at this register subordinates everything to one thing. The current header subordinates nothing.

## Part 2 — Synthesis

### What converts a single-SKU header from "label on a box" to "moment that earns the page below"

Three observations from the research, ranked by load-bearing weight.

1. **Pick a protagonist; let the other three recede.** Across every fetched reference, the working pattern is *one* element at scale and the others as captions. Patek lets the reference number be the headline; Le Labo lets the name; Aksum lets the brand mark; Liga Privada lets the tagline; Glenfiddich lets the bottle; Plasencia lets the photograph. The current Volage header asks the reader to register edition + name + vitola + tagline + cigar image, all simultaneously, with no instruction on hierarchy. The eye finds nowhere to land. **Mariposa's strongest protagonists in priority order:** (a) the tagline, which is one of the sharpest lines on the entire site; (b) the cigar itself, which is photographically strong and brand-distinctive; (c) the name "Volage", which is short, lyrical, and unfamiliar enough to carry display scale; (d) the gold leaf, which is the brand's actual signature and currently lives in section 2.

2. **The gold rectangular frame is a card frame.** Same diagnosis as the product-image research: the 1:1 gold-bordered media frame reads as a marketing container rather than as an editorial object. Premium SKU pages bound their hero images with one of three devices: full-bleed (no frame), or a contained image on a wider quiet ground (no frame, just space), or a deliberate *crop* with no frame (Davidoff's humidor scene, the watch-dial macro). None of them put the hero image inside a rectangle that announces "this is the image area." The frame is doing what whitespace should do.

3. **The header is a *single beat*, not a four-line stack.** Splits work when one column carries the meaning and the other carries the object. They fail when both columns are doing equal work and the type column itself is doing four jobs (eyebrow + name + vitola + tagline). The Aman split works because the type column has *one* protagonist (the property name) and three subordinated captions (location, descriptor, body). The current Volage split has four near-equally-weighted lines and reads as a label, not a statement.

### The shape of the right answer

Four viable directions, each picking a different protagonist and a different compositional grammar. All four preserve the existing information (First Edition / Volage / Canonazo / tagline + image). All four remove or subordinate the gold rectangular frame. All four honour Spectral italic + Hanken Grotesk, the palette tokens, no exclamation marks, no em dashes. Stephane picks one.

## Part 3 — Four concrete options for Volage

### Option A — The tagline as the protagonist (recommended)

**Name.** Tagline-led. Or: the Liga Privada move.
**Description.** Demote the type stack and the image, promote the tagline. The header becomes a near-full-viewport editorial pause where *"A cigar that doesn't wait for an occasion."* sits as the single display-scale line, with the other three pieces of metadata (edition, name, vitola) tucked beneath it as a small composed caption, and the cigar image present but quieter — either to one side, or as a tall narrow object on a generous cream ground.

**Composition spec.**
- Section height: 88–95vh (commits the viewport on entry).
- Background: `--pearl` or `--cream` ground. No frame, no border.
- Layout: split at desktop, but the split is *uneven* — type column occupies ~62% of the width, image column ~38%. The visual centre of gravity is the tagline, not the geometric centre of the page.
- Image column: the existing `cigar-volage.png` (848 × 3334, isolated cigar on transparent ground, already in the asset library) at full column height, set ~60–70% of column width, centred horizontally, no frame, with a soft directional drop shadow.
- Type column, top: a small composed eyebrow combining the two metadata lines on one row — `FIRST EDITION  ·  CANONAZO` — in Hanken Grotesk uppercase, `--gold-deep`, tracked, the two pieces separated by a tracked dot.
- Type column, middle: a single thin gold-leaf hairline (1px, `--gold-leaf`, ~120px wide), as a typographic ornament.
- Type column, headline: the tagline, in Spectral italic, clamp(2.4rem, 5vw, 4.2rem), `--ink`, on a 22ch measure, line-height 1.05. The hanging opening quote is unset; clean run of italic.
- Type column, foot: the SKU name "Volage" in Spectral roman, small (clamp(1rem, 1.6vw, 1.3rem)), `--ink`, treated as a signature beneath the tagline rather than as a headline above it.

**Image-side spec.** Existing `cigar-volage.png` works as-is. The PNG already has the right shape and an alpha channel. The only adjustment is removing the gold border and the 1:1 aspect ratio that currently contains it.

**Type ladder (top to bottom, type-column).**
1. `FIRST EDITION · CANONAZO` — Hanken Grotesk uppercase, fs-eyebrow, gold-deep, tracked.
2. (gold hairline ornament)
3. ***A cigar that doesn't wait for an occasion.*** — Spectral italic, display clamp, ink, 22ch.
4. *Volage* — Spectral roman, body scale, ink, treated as signature.

**Motion.** Eyebrow fades in first (0.7s, no rise). Hairline draws left-to-right (0.6s, ease-out, delayed 0.15s). Tagline fades up with 28px rise (0.95s, ease-out, delayed 0.25s). SKU name fades up with 16px rise (0.7s, delayed 0.55s). Cigar image fades + scales 1.02 → 1.0 (1.1s, delayed 0.1s). Total entry cascade ~1.2s; restrained, editorial.

**Photography required.** None — uses the existing `cigar-volage.png`. *(Caprice equivalent is `cigar-caprice.png`, already in the library; same template applies cleanly to /caprice.)*

**Why recommended.** Three reasons. (a) The tagline is the strongest single sentence on the entire Mariposa site and is currently buried as line four in a stack of four. Promoting it costs nothing and pays out immediately. (b) The existing isolated-cigar PNG is already the asset this option needs; no new photography required. (c) The composition reads as editorial-pause rather than DTC-template — the rhythm of the page below (signature → narrative → specs → smoke arc → packaging → cross-link) is set up by a header that *commits a moment* rather than a header that *labels a product*. Closest match to the patterns Liga Privada, Aksum, Roman & Williams, and Diptyque all converge on. Pick.

### Option B — The full-bleed Davidoff room

**Name.** Photographic room. Or: the Winston Churchill move.
**Description.** Replace the framed image and the type stack with a full-bleed photographic hero — the existing `cigar-volage-pink.jpg` (or `cigar-volage-woman.jpg`) extended edge-to-edge of the viewport as a *scene*, with the four pieces of metadata laid over the photograph in a small, composed caption block at the bottom-left or bottom-right corner. The cigar lives in its scene; the type is an overlay credit. The hero is an atmosphere first, an object second, a label third.

**Composition spec.**
- Section height: ~92vh (full viewport on entry, breathing room above the contracted nav).
- Background: full-bleed photograph, edge-to-edge, no container, no frame. `cigar-volage-pink.jpg` re-cropped to landscape (current 1:1 cropped to 16:9 or 21:9), with the cigar weighted to one third of the frame and ambient pink / cream space taking the other two thirds. A subtle dark-to-clear gradient (10–20% opacity, anchored to the type's corner) maintains type legibility against the photographic ground.
- Type block sits in one corner — recommend bottom-left, with the type aligned to a generous left margin and a small bottom margin. The composition reads as *signature on a print*.
- Type block content (vertically stacked, tight rhythm):
  - Eyebrow: `FIRST EDITION` — Hanken, uppercase, gold-leaf (on dark image areas) or gold-deep (on lighter), tracked.
  - Name: "Volage" — Spectral display, clamp(2.6rem, 4.8vw, 3.6rem), light (cream/pearl) against the photographic ground.
  - Vitola: `CANONAZO` — Hanken uppercase, small, gold-leaf, tracked, sitting tight under the name.
  - Tagline: *A cigar that doesn't wait for an occasion.* — Spectral italic, clamp(1.1rem, 1.6vw, 1.35rem), cream, with a short line measure (~38ch) and a slight margin-top from the vitola.

**Image-side spec.** Existing `cigar-volage-pink.jpg` (1392 × 1392) is workable but tight; ideally re-crop to landscape, or commission a wider scene shot in the same register. If `cigar-volage-woman.jpg` is preferred for editorial mood, that is the alternate ground.

**Type ladder.**
1. `FIRST EDITION` — Hanken eyebrow.
2. *Volage* — Spectral display, light on dark photographic ground.
3. `CANONAZO` — Hanken caption.
4. *A cigar that doesn't wait for an occasion.* — Spectral italic, light, short measure.

**Motion.** Image is static on load. Type block fades up with a 22px rise as a single composed unit (0.95s, no internal stagger — the block is a single object). Optional subtle parallax on the image (1.03 → 1.0 over the first scroll) — *skip if it feels noisy*.

**Photography required.** Existing assets usable as v1 (re-crop required). Stronger v2: a commissioned landscape scene shot — cigar in context, ambient pink / cream environment, generous negative space on the type side. Production: one half-day shoot.

**Why not recommended (but strong second).** Highest immediate impact of the four — a full-bleed photographic hero is the move every reference brand makes when they have the photography to back it. Holds back from a default recommendation for two reasons. (a) The existing assets are square and will need re-cropping or reshooting to read at full-bleed landscape — small but real production cost. (b) The page below already has heavy editorial weight (the signature section, the smoke arc), and a full-bleed photographic hero on top can read as *too much atmosphere* before the reader gets to the substance. If Stephane wants pure cinematic register and is willing to commission a landscape shot, this is the option. The risk in Option A (less immediate impact) is real but lower than the risk in B (asset gap + tonal heaviness).

### Option C — The deferred hero (eyebrow header, then signature)

**Name.** Deferred hero. Or: the Aman-with-location-eyebrow move, tightened.
**Description.** Keep the split, but invert the hierarchy and demote the header to a *compact* opening block. The name "Volage" sits as a tightly-composed display line with a paired eyebrow above it (`FIRST EDITION · CANONAZO`), the cigar image quietly to one side, and the tagline pulled out as a separate *pull-quote* moment immediately below the header block. The header itself takes only 55–60vh; the tagline gets a small (15–20vh) display pause of its own beneath it. Two small moments rather than one big one.

**Composition spec.**
- Header section: 55–60vh, two-column split, image left ~38% of width, type right ~52%.
- Image: existing `cigar-volage.png` (isolated cigar PNG), tall, ~80% of column height, no frame, soft drop shadow.
- Type column, top: eyebrow `FIRST EDITION · CANONAZO` (Hanken uppercase, gold-deep, tracked, single row).
- Type column, middle: "Volage" in Spectral display, clamp(3.6rem, 7vw, 5.4rem), tighter line-height (0.92), with a hanging italic *V* if the typeface supports it tastefully (test before commit).
- A 1px gold-leaf hairline, ~140px, sits below the name as the section's typographic foot.
- Pull-quote section immediately below: ~18vh, full-bleed cream/pearl, centred, single line of Spectral italic at clamp(1.6rem, 3vw, 2.4rem) — *"A cigar that doesn't wait for an occasion."* — `--ink`, 30ch max, with a tiny hanging opening quote mark.

**Image-side spec.** Same `cigar-volage.png` as Option A.

**Type ladder.**
1. `FIRST EDITION · CANONAZO` — eyebrow.
2. *Volage* — Spectral display, large.
3. (gold hairline ornament)
4. (small vertical gap, deliberate)
5. *"A cigar that doesn't wait for an occasion."* — Spectral italic pull-quote, centred, in its own sub-section.

**Motion.** Header reveals as a single block (fade + 22px rise, 0.9s). Hairline draws after type lands (0.5s). Pull-quote sub-section has its own reveal on scroll (rise + fade, 1s).

**Photography required.** None new.

**Why not recommended.** Compositionally distinct — it splits the hero into two beats — but adds a section to a page that already has six. The tagline gets its own moment, which is good editorially, but the header's compactness can read as truncated rather than restrained on viewports that have used to a near-full-viewport hero. Holds. Worth considering if Stephane wants the *most* editorial of the four (two beats > one beat) and is willing to spend the vertical real estate.

### Option D — The typographic pause (image-less)

**Name.** Typographic pause. Or: the Aesop / Patek 5236 move with the cigar removed entirely.
**Description.** Remove the cigar image from the header. The header becomes a pure typographic moment on cream — eyebrow, name at display scale, vitola caption, tagline as a closing italic line — composed as a centred editorial title page. The cigar image is *deferred to the signature section below*, where it sits more naturally (and where there is already a placeholder slot). The header earns its place by being a *pause*, not a *product card*.

**Composition spec.**
- Section height: ~65–75vh. Generous top and bottom padding.
- Background: pure `--pearl` or `--cream`, edge-to-edge. No image, no texture, no card.
- Layout: centred column, max-width ~720px, all type elements centred horizontally.
- Top: eyebrow `FIRST EDITION` — Hanken uppercase, small, gold-deep, tracked.
- Below: name "Volage" in Spectral display, clamp(4.4rem, 9vw, 7rem), tight line-height, `--ink`. Goes large — bigger than any of the other three options put it.
- Below: vitola `CANONAZO` — Hanken uppercase, small, gold-deep, tracked, sits ~12–16px under the name.
- Below: a 1px gold-leaf hairline, ~80px, centred.
- Below: tagline *A cigar that doesn't wait for an occasion.* — Spectral italic, clamp(1.2rem, 2vw, 1.5rem), `--ink`, 28ch max, centred.

**Image-side spec.** No image in header. The cigar image moves to the signature section's open placeholder slot, or paired with the signature copy as a small contextual image.

**Type ladder.**
1. `FIRST EDITION` — eyebrow.
2. *Volage* — Spectral display, very large.
3. `CANONAZO` — caption.
4. (gold hairline)
5. *A cigar that doesn't wait for an occasion.* — Spectral italic, body-display scale.

**Motion.** Quiet sequential reveal on the four elements (eyebrow → name → vitola → hairline → tagline), 80–120ms staggers, 0.7–0.9s durations each. Minimal — the moment is the type, not the motion.

**Photography required.** None for the header. The cigar image migrates to section 2 (signature), where the placeholder slot already exists.

**Why not recommended.** Editorially honest and aesthetically pure, but two real risks. (a) Removes the cigar from the page's opening moment, which is a positioning choice rather than a design choice — the cigar disappearing from the header means the page's first photographic moment is the (currently placeholder) signature section, which puts a lot of weight on commissioning the gold-leaf macro Section 2 needs. (b) Asks Mariposa to compete with Aesop and Patek on type alone, which requires Spectral display at the larger sizes to hold its weight. Test before commit — at clamp(4.4–7rem), Spectral italic may hint awkwardly on some renderers. Worth Yulia mocking; if it lands and Section 2's macro is commissioned in parallel, this becomes the most editorial of the four. If the macro is delayed, this option breaks the page below.

### Quick comparison

| Option | Protagonist | Image treatment | Photography needed | Section height | Risk | Editorial register |

### Recommended pick

**Option A — tagline-led, with the existing isolated cigar PNG on a cream ground.** Promotes the strongest single sentence on the site, removes the rectangular frame, requires no new photography, commits the viewport, and reads as the same editorial register the rest of the page is being rebuilt toward. The four-line type stack becomes a one-line headline with three small captions around it — exactly the hierarchy every reference brand in this category uses.

## Part 4 — Implementation notes for Yulia

**Stack.** Existing Astro project, GSAP available, `reveal()` helper in `src/lib/motion.ts`. None of the four options requires new dependencies. The existing `/volage` hero is inline in `src/pages/volage.astro` (lines 67–95 in the current build). Refactor in place; do not componentise yet (Caprice will reuse, but the cleanest moment to extract is after Stephane signs the chosen option).

**Per-option build notes:**

- **Option A (recommended).** Strip `.hero__media-frame` border, background, aspect ratio, and the gold rectangular bound entirely. Keep `.hero__media` as a column wrapper sized at ~38% of the grid. Render the existing `cigar-volage.png` (not `.jpg`) directly inside it; PNG alpha channel + soft CSS drop-shadow (`filter: drop-shadow(0 24px 40px rgba(31, 27, 23, 0.18))`) replaces the frame. ## What did I miss?

Three honest gaps.

1. **Macallan, Hermès, Vacheron, IWC, A. The *direction* the category takes is well-supported by the thirteen verified sources; the specific compositional details of the blocked sites are inferred from prior research and category press. If Stephane wants me to push past the blocks with screen-grabs or a different fetch tool, I will. The four options would not change.

2. **I haven't seen the live `/volage` page rendered.** I read `volage.astro` directly (the current header is on lines 67–95) and confirmed the asset library, but I haven't pulled up the live page to verify the visual impression in context of the contracted nav above and the signature section below. The structural analysis is sound; a 30-second look would let me confirm the section-height proposals (88–95vh in A and B; 55–60vh in C; 65–75vh in D) play correctly against the nav's contracted state and the page's existing rhythm.

3. **The four options optimise for editorial register at the cost of a fifth option that exists in the category but doesn't fit Mariposa.** That fifth option is *animated / video hero* — Glenfiddich Grande Couronne runs a hero video, Patrón runs a Guillermo del Toro short, Davidoff cigar of the year animates. I omitted it deliberately because (a) Mariposa has no motion content and commissioning is six figures, (b) the brand's editorial restraint reads as more credible than motion-heavy heroes at the small-brand stage, and (c) two of the four options above already commit the viewport, which is the genuine win that video heroes deliver. If Stephane disagrees on any of those three premises, the option set should add a video variant. Holding unless asked.

**One question Stephane was not expecting.** The current header places the SKU name "Volage" at display scale and the tagline ("A cigar that doesn't wait for an occasion.") at body scale. Three of the four options above invert that — promoting the tagline, demoting the name. The instinct under that inversion is *the tagline is the strongest sentence on the site and the name is short and lyrical enough to live as a signature*. But the inversion is also a positioning bet: it says Mariposa wants to lead with *brand voice*, not with *SKU identity*. Question: is the SKU name's prominence a non-negotiable Stephane wants preserved (Option D and the "Volage as `h1` display" variant), or is he comfortable with the brand's voice being the headline and the SKU name being the signature (Options A, B, C)? The recommendation assumes the latter; the right answer might be different.
