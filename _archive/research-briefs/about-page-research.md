# About page — research brief and four full-page proposals

**Author.** Pax.
**Date.** 2026-05-24.
**Status.** First-pass deliverable for Stephane. Yulia is the intended builder.
**Brief.** `/about` currently exists as a placeholder reusing the `<OriginArc />` component (three beats: Argopuro / Jember / Time) so the content was not orphaned when Stephane lifted Origin off /volage. Mariposa now needs a real About page — distributor-primary, the credibility payload for the brand: twenty-year acclimatised Havana seed, 24-month fermentation, hand-rolling, draw-testing, the founder story, the gold-leaf signature. The brand pager (`mariposa - brand (2026-05-20).html`) carries the canonical Origins and Story copy that this page is built from. This document investigates how premium and adjacent brands handle the About / Heritage / Story page, then proposes four full-page layouts and recommends one.

The canonical copy on the brand pager is unrewritten throughout — verbatim extracts only. Axel handles any line-level polish later. Trade terms (escogida, entubado, torcedoras, Canonazo) stay unglossed.

## Part 1 — Research

Sixteen primary URLs queued; ten returned usable visual / structural descriptions, six were 403 / 404 / 503 / SSL or DNS failure (Macallan whisky-makers, Hennessy The House, Aesop our-story, Diptyque our-story EU, Frédéric Malle about, Davidoff Geneva root). Where direct fetch failed I leant on the prior three rounds of research in this folder and on analyst / press write-ups; those substitutions are flagged inline. Confidence on the *direction* the category takes is high (the consensus is strong across the verified sources); confidence on specific details of bot-blocked sites is medium per site.

The single most useful observation up front, so the rest reads as evidence: **at this register, the About page is rarely a "company brochure".** It is almost always one of three things — a chronology (Glenfiddich, Plasencia, Davidoff Heritage), a manifesto (Le Labo, Aesop), or a place-and-people atmosphere (Aman, Soori, Drew Estate's Our Story). What it is almost *never* is a generic "Our Mission / Our Values / Our Team" stack with a stock founder portrait. The cigar category's strongest About pages (Drew Estate, Plasencia, Foundation) all commit to one of the three forms hard. The weakest (Davidoff's compartmentalised Discover/Heritage routing) try to do all three at once and dissolve.

### Premium spirits — chronology as the spine

**Glenfiddich — Our Story** *(direct fetch).* A long-form, scroll-driven chronological narrative anchored on William Grant and the family. Opens with founder positioning ("In the summer of 1886, William Grant started building his Distillery by hand with his seven sons and two daughters"), then a year-by-year timeline 1887 → 2021 with image-to-text ratio close to 1:1 per milestone, each milestone year + descriptive paragraph + documentary photograph. Closes with a "Where Next?" product strip. Heritage is the *entire architecture* of the page. No motion beyond standard scroll. *Lesson:* a chronology only works when the brand has a hundred-plus years of milestones to plot. Mariposa does not. But the *device* — a single anchoring date (1859 Jember cigar-making heritage, or Pak Abdul Kahar Muzakir's twenty-year acclimatisation programme) presented as a fixed historical pivot — is portable.

**Macallan — Whisky Makers / The Distillery** *(403 in this round; covered in the Volage and story-section briefs).* Full-bleed photography of the Highland landscape, single italic display lines as section openers, the *place* (Easter Elchies, the River Spey) is the protagonist. Founder gets one paragraph; the land gets pages. *Confidence medium — direct fetch failed.*

**Hennessy — The House** *(503 in this round).* Category convention from prior research: cinematic full-bleed video / large stills, single tight lines of type over photographic grounds, the prose lives a click deeper. *Confidence medium — direct fetch failed.*

### Premium cigar — three viable templates

**Drew Estate — Our Story** *(direct fetch).* Long-form scrollytelling, nine named sections: *The Movement Begins → New York Roots → Our Early Brands → Reinventing Nicaragua → Back Stateside → La Gran Fabrica → The Rebirth*. Each section pairs 3-6 paragraphs of first-person founder narrative with a multi-image carousel (carousels run from 4 to 26 photos). A single attributed pull-quote from Jonathan Drew sits in the Nicaragua section. Heritage / craft / place / founder all earn their space because the brand has a *story* to tell — the Brooklyn-to-Estelí arc, the Hurricane Mitch financial collapse, the rebirth. *Lesson:* this is the template Mariposa is closest to in structural shape, but only if the founder narrative carries the same density. Stephane's discovery-in-Java arc is the equivalent beat, but it is one chapter, not nine. The Drew Estate model run at Mariposa's content scale would feel padded.

**Plasencia — Heritage** *(direct fetch).* A pure chronological timeline 1865 → 2022, each era a distinct section, one large historical photograph + 15-40 words of caption per entry. Family names anchor each turning point (Don Eduardo Plasencia, Sixto Plasencia Juares, Sixto Plasencia Pérez, Néstor Plasencia, Néstor Andrés). Visually image-dominant, text deliberately brief. The chronology *is* the page. *Lesson:* Plasencia gets to do this because they have 160 harvests and six generations to plot. Mariposa has roughly twenty years of acclimatisation and one founder. The chronology format would either collapse or pad. Holds, but informs the "one anchoring date" device used in Option C below.

**Davidoff — Heritage** *(direct fetch).* A horizontally-scrolling carousel of historical milestone images with "Discover More" expandable buttons per entry. The page is structured as an *index* of stories rather than a single read. Founder treatment (Zino Davidoff, Dr. Ernst Schneider) lives behind the expandable buttons. The pattern works for Davidoff's hundred years of milestones but reads as a database of heritage rather than a narrative. *Lesson:* compartmentalising the story behind expandable buttons saves real estate but at the cost of editorial register. Avoid for Mariposa.

**Foundation Cigar Co. — About** *(direct fetch).* Single dominant founder section: Nicholas Melillo treated as "Founder & Master Blender", one large hero portrait, ~300 words of biographical narrative emphasising 20+ years in the industry and his Connecticut-to-Nicaragua arc. Below the bio, a four-portrait gallery. Image-to-text ratio approximately 1:4 favouring prose. Place treatment: Connecticut as origin, Nicaragua as formation. *Lesson:* the most cigar-specific About template — *founder as protagonist, place as formative, craft as outcome*. Closest in structural shape to what Mariposa needs, though Melillo-style heavy founder centring would push too hard on Stephane in a way the brand voice has so far avoided.

**Padrón — About** *(404 in this round; covered in prior briefs).* Pure catalogue / family-name listing, almost no editorial register. The weakest reference in the cigar set.

### Premium fragrance — manifesto over biography

**Le Labo — About Us** *(direct fetch).* A *manifesto*. Opens with 14 "We believe..." declarations rather than a founder biography. Voice is irreverent and declarative ("we can be immature forever", "explanation kills art"). Below the manifesto: a *Labs & Souls* section with minimal text and film links, a *Craftspeople* carousel (seven location-based profiles rotating through Australia, France, Italy, US, Japan), a *Collections & Roots* closer with a single historical line — "Le Labo launched in 2006 at 233 Elizabeth Street in Nolita, NYC" — and the founders mentioned only as *"two friends wanting to create a scented revolution"*. **No founder names, no founder portraits.** The brand voice carries the whole page. *Lesson:* the most editorially confident About page in the fetched set. Mariposa cannot run the manifesto register without Axel, but the underlying move — *let the voice do the founder work* — is the cleanest in the category.

**Aesop — Our Story** *(403 in this round; covered in prior briefs).* Category convention: white ground, generous space, body prose at tight measure, no founder portrait at hero, brand story routed across several short essays in The Fabulist rather than consolidated. The page itself is the frame. *Confidence medium — direct fetch failed; underlying pattern is the most consistent thing about the Aesop reference set across rounds.*

**Frédéric Malle — About** *(connection refused this round).* Category convention from prior research: founder Frédéric Malle is named and credited as the editor-perfumer, but the page foregrounds the *perfumer-as-author* model (each fragrance has a named perfumer treated like a film director) rather than Malle's biography. *Confidence medium — direct fetch failed.*

### Lifestyle and hospitality — atmosphere as story

**Aman — About Us** *(direct fetch).* Long-form text-heavy About page, ~4000-5000px scroll length, seven major sections: *brand origin → service philosophy → current portfolio (36 properties) → future pipeline → lifestyle products → Janu sub-brand → Aman at Sea*. Image-to-prose ratio roughly 30% visual / 70% textual — meditative dense prose punctuated by atmospheric stills (a musician at Amanbagh, aerial camp views, temple silhouettes). Leadership minimised: Chairman / CEO Vlad Doronin mentioned once in a forward-looking context, no biographical narrative. Block descriptive passages as thematic anchors ("Time with Aman is akin to being welcomed into a gracious private home where only a few guests are received"). *Lesson:* place and philosophy as the protagonists; founder receded almost entirely. The exact inverse of Foundation Cigar's About. Most editorial of the set after Le Labo. Pages this long only work when the brand has 36 properties to weave; Mariposa would need to compress the form.

**Soori Bali — About** *(direct fetch).* Five modular content blocks below a video-hero (Story of Soori, Interiors, Location, Facilities, Sustainability), each pairing high-quality imagery with a few sentences of concise copy, each with a "More Details" link to a sub-page. Founder Soo K. Chan is named explicitly and treated as designer-owner-operator. The story-as-private-home origin (the resort built first as Chan's family vacation home, then opened to the public) is foregrounded and the credibility comes from the architect being the proprietor. Moderate scroll. *Lesson:* the most useful single hospitality reference in this round because Bali-adjacent and structurally compact. Five blocks + founder positioning + photography is roughly the shape Mariposa needs. Place (Bali, ocean, Mount Batukaru, rice terraces) sits alongside founder; neither dominates.

**Roman and Williams Guild — About** *(direct fetch).* Two large Cass Bird hero photographs open the page, then a single long-form prose section: founders Robin and Stephen Alesch introduced (2002 founding, names of maternal grandfathers, fine art / architecture / cinema backgrounds), brand philosophy ("treatment of projects as narratives, historical investigation filtered through contemporary sensibility"), institutional credentials (Metropolitan Museum's British Galleries, The Standard High Line, Le Coucou, Ace Hotels), retail expansion (Guild Shop, La Mercerie, Guild Gallery), and a closing statement (50 people, seven countries). One quoted line from the founders sits prominently across the homepage / brand wrapper. No motion. *Lesson:* the most prose-heavy founder-centric About in the fetched set, and it works because the founders' credentials are heavy enough to anchor the prose. Mariposa's founder credentials are *different* — one founder, one origin, one acclimatisation programme — so the prose volume is smaller; the same structural shape compressed.

**Singita — Our Story** *(connection refused this round).* Held; category convention covered in prior research (place-first, conservation-and-craft narrative).

### Editorial design — what holds the four together

Across every fetched source above the pattern is consistent: an About page at this register picks *one* mode and commits to it. The four working modes:

1. **Chronology** (Glenfiddich, Plasencia, Davidoff Heritage). Needs deep history.
2. **Manifesto** (Le Labo, Aesop). Needs editorial voice strong enough to carry without biography.
3. **Founder-as-protagonist** (Foundation, Roman & Williams, Soori). Needs a founder who earns the spotlight.
4. **Place-and-philosophy** (Aman, Macallan, Hennessy). Needs a single place and a brand voice meditative enough to carry mood-prose.

The About page that fails is the one that mixes three modes lukewarmly. The Mariposa About page should pick one and lean.

## Part 2 — Synthesis

### What converts a distributor-facing About from "company brochure" to "brand artefact"

A distributor lands on `/about` making a credibility judgment on three things, in order:

1. **The brand is real and has a point of view.** Editorial voice, restrained design, signature element pulled forward. Manifesto pages (Le Labo) and place-and-philosophy pages (Aman) both deliver this on register alone. The Davidoff Heritage compartmentalisation actively undermines it.
2. **The cigar is technically credible.** The acclimatised Havana seed, the 24-month fermentation, the entubado bunching, the triple cap, the draw-testing. These need to be on the page but not as a spec sheet — as the *evidence* under the brand voice.
3. **The brand is led by someone who knows what they are doing.** Founder treatment is the slot where a distributor either decides this is a real outfit or a marketing exercise. Foundation Cigar's bio-portrait template is overdone for Mariposa; Aman's almost-no-founder is too sparse for a brand of two cigars; Roman & Williams' compressed prose-with-quote is roughly right.

A distributor scanning the page in 30 seconds gets the *voice* and the *signature*. A distributor reading it top-to-bottom gets *founder + place + craft + ageing + signature* without ever encountering a spec table.

A connoisseur reading it gets the unglossed trade terms (escogida, entubado, torcedoras, Canonazo, Cuban triple cap) treated as a given, the volcanic-soil-and-Havana-seed claim treated as substantive rather than ornamental, and Stephane's voice (or a credible third-person account of him) running underneath.

### What does NOT belong on this page

- **Customer testimonials.** No distributor reviews to quote pre-launch. A connoisseur quote pre-launch would read as fabricated. Hold for v2 after first reviews land.
- **Mission-statement filler.** "Our mission is to bring..." / "Our values are..." constructions belong in a corporate deck, not a brand artefact. Le Labo's manifesto works because it has voice; a generic mission statement would not.
- **Generic founder pose.** A staged founder portrait in a guayabera with a cigar would actively damage the register. If a founder image runs, it is documentary (founder in the field, founder with the rollers, founder with the leaf) and ideally B&W.
- **A team page or a Meet The Founders grid.** Mariposa is a one-founder brand. Pluralising it as a team would dilute the protagonism.
- **A press / awards strip.** None to run yet. Hold for v2.
- **A timeline grid of milestones.** No history deep enough to justify it. Forcing a four-event timeline (2024 founded / 2025 first blend / 2026 launch) would read as padding.
- **A spec table.** Specs belong on the SKU pages. The About page communicates the *process* (24-month fermentation, hand-rolling, draw-testing) as narrative, not as data.
- **A "Sustainability" or "Responsibility" block.** Honest position only — Mariposa has not yet earned the right to claim sustainability credentials at scale. A single line on the female-roller team (mirroring Cuba's torcedoras, families that have worked tobacco for generations) belongs in the craft section, not as its own "social impact" block.
- **A contact form.** Contact lives in the footer or a dedicated `/contact` page if needed. Forms break the editorial register.
- **A newsletter signup.** Same. Global footer.

### What signals "company brochure" vs "brand artefact"

| Signal | Brochure | Artefact |
|---|---|---|
| Opening | "Welcome to..." / generic hero with logo | A line, a place, a date, or a single image with weight |
| Voice | Marketing-team neutral | Authorial, restrained, opinionated |
| Founder | Staged portrait + bio paragraph | Documentary image, named, narrated quietly |
| Process | Bullets and labels | Prose, ungroossed terms, time and place specified |
| Photography | Stock and staged | Documentary, B&W, hands, leaf, place |
| Closing | Contact form / CTA grid | A single line that lands |

### The shape of the right answer

Four viable directions, each picking a different mode and a different organising spine. All four honour the brand vocabulary (Spectral italic + Hanken Grotesk, palette tokens, no exclamation marks, no em dashes, trade terms unglossed). All four use the canonical brand-pager copy as the source text. Stephane picks one.

## Part 3 — Four concrete About-page proposals for Mariposa

### Option A — *The Founder's Letter* (recommended)

**Description.** A founder-centric editorial page, structured around Stephane's discovery-in-Java arc but told in a voice quiet enough to feel like a letter. The signature (24-carat gold leaf, Wrights of Lymm pedigree if the supplier is confirmed) gets its own moment; the craft (Pak Abdul Kahar Muzakir, 20-year acclimatisation, 24-month fermentation, female torcedora team, entubado, triple cap, draw-test) gets its own section; the place (Jember, Argopuro) gets its own atmospheric beat. Roughly six sections including header and closing, fits comfortably on a single scroll, reads as one continuous editorial rather than a stack of CMS blocks.

This is the Roman & Williams / Foundation / Soori register, compressed for Mariposa's single-founder single-place scope, with a Macallan-style place-photograph weight in the middle. The closest cigar-category analogue is Foundation's About, restrained editorially toward Aesop.

**Section-by-section.**

**1. Header — *The mariposa flower* (full-bleed pearl ground, type-led).** ~75vh. The page opens not with the founder, not with a date, not with a hero shot, but with the *story behind the name*. Single eyebrow `MARIPOSA` (Hanken, gold-deep, tracked) → display-italic Spectral line lifted verbatim from the brand pager: *"The white butterfly ginger became Cuba's national flower in 1936."* (clamp 2.4-3.6rem, 28ch). → small italic body paragraph below, also verbatim: *"During Cuba's wars of independence, women slipped secret messages for the guerrilleros into the petals of the Mariposa flower."* → a thin gold-leaf hairline at the foot of the section. No image. The text is the protagonist.

*Composition.* Centred column, max 720px, pearl `--pearl` ground, no card. Generous vertical padding. The nav adopts cream-over-cream behaviour here (the cream observer hook is set on this section, same as the homepage product slider).

*Photography required.* None.

*Reuse.* Net-new component or extension of the existing position-statement primitive.

**2. *A quiet signature* (white ground, two-column, image-led).** The gold leaf, before the founder. Lifted directly from the brand pager: *"Mariposa cigars carry a secret message of their own. Instead of a traditional printed band, a 24-carat gold leaf is laid onto the wrapper by hand by Javanese women. It is a discreet reminder that the finer things shouldn't be saved for a special occasion. They are meant to be enjoyed today."* — set as a single display-italic lede + a short body paragraph in Hanken (the Wrights of Lymm credit only if the supplier is confirmed by Stephane; otherwise omit). Paired with a macro photograph of the gold leaf on the cigar, ideally application in progress.

*Composition.* `<SignatureBlock />` reused as-is. Pearl ground stays white here so the section sits as a quieter beat between the pearl bookends.

*Photography required.* **YES.** Macro shot of 24-carat gold leaf on Mariposa cigar, application in progress or freshly applied. This is the same shot Section 2 of /volage already needs — one commission, two placements.

*Reuse.* `<SignatureBlock />` direct. Placeholder slot already wired.

**3. *The founder* (cream `--cream #f2f0e9` ground, two-column, type-dominant).** The discovery-in-Java arc, verbatim from the brand pager's *Story* section: *"Mariposa's story begins with a journey. Driven by a lifelong taste for cigars, founder Stephane Zermatten followed his curiosity to the tobacco fields of East Java."* → *"What he discovered inspired him to craft a blend for his own humidor and closest friends. Yet, as word spread and a wider circle grew curious, he knew it couldn't remain a private reserve. The brand was born to bring this unique cigar to a growing community in Bali and beyond."* → *"Today, Mariposa captures the rich, multi-cultural soul of cigars, giving friends around the world the perfect excuse to light up, slow down, and share a good conversation. Whatever the hour."*

A single small documentary image to one side (founder in the field at Jember, or founder with Pak Abdul Kahar Muzakir, or founder with the rollers). Image is portrait-orientation, ~3:4, ~38% of the column width. No staged founder pose. B&W preferred over colour to set the documentary register apart from the brand pager's colour photography elsewhere.

A small *attribution caption* below the image — "Stephane Zermatten · Jember, East Java" in Hanken small, gold-deep. The caption does the founder-naming work so the prose can stay third-person.

*Composition.* Cream ground (`--cream #f2f0e9`) anchors this section as the page's central editorial pause; the nav cream-observer adopts here. Two-column: prose left (~58%), image right (~38%) at desktop, stacked on mobile.

*Photography required.* **YES.** One founder documentary shot. Existing photography library has no founder image suitable; this needs commissioning or surfacing from Stephane's archive. If unavailable for v1, ship with the placeholder pattern (corner ticks + caption "PLACEHOLDER: founder portrait, Jember"); the section still reads.

*Reuse.* New layout but uses existing eyebrow + body primitives. Placeholder pattern reusable from `<SignatureBlock />`.

**4. *Origin — the soil, the seed, the workshop* (pearl ground, three-beat).** The existing OriginArc, reused as-is. Three beats (Argopuro / Jember / Time) with their verbatim copy from `volage.json#origin`. This is the *place-and-process* section — volcanic earth, Havana seed acclimatised twenty years, hand-rolled by torcedoras, entubado, three-part construction, Cuban triple cap, 24 months of fermentation, 12 months of ageing. The brand pager's *Origins* paragraphs supply the language; the OriginArc's three beats are the visual structure.

The brand pager's `Origins` section has a denser version — four substantive paragraphs covering Pak Abdul Kahar Muzakir, the acclimatisation, the escogida fermentation, the female roller team, the entubado / triple-cap construction, and the per-stick draw-test. The denser version is the credibility payload distributors actually need. Two ways to handle, decision for Stephane:
- *(a) Three-beat OriginArc as today, with a small expandable "Read the full origin" disclosure that reveals the brand-pager paragraphs in-place.*
- *(b) Replace the three-beat OriginArc on this page with a longer prose treatment of the four brand-pager paragraphs — keep the OriginArc for /volage and /caprice where its visual rhythm earns its space.*

*Recommend (b).* On the About page the credibility paragraphs do more work than the visual rhythm; the three-beat OriginArc reads correctly as a SKU-page sequence but reads as too compressed on a heritage page. Reserve OriginArc for the SKU pages.

*Composition.* If (b), this becomes a *dense prose* section — eyebrow `ORIGINS`, then the four brand-pager paragraphs verbatim, set in body Hanken at body scale on a 64ch measure, centred, with three small inline images interrupting the prose (Argopuro / Jember / Time — same three slots, now framing rather than driving). Pearl ground for warmth and to mark the section's weight.

*Photography required.* **YES — three.** Argopuro range or volcanic field, Jember rolling room (torcedoras at work — *the* shot if only one is feasible), ageing room. All three placeholders are already documented in `volage.json#origin`. Single shoot or commission from BIN's archive.

*Reuse.* If (a): `<OriginArc />` direct + a new `<details>` disclosure. If (b): new `<OriginProse />` component or extend `<SignatureBlock />` to accept multiple inline images. Recommend (b) and extending an existing primitive.

**5. *The promise* (white ground, single-line pull-quote).** A typographic pause. The brand pager's `Story` section closes on this kicker, lifted verbatim: *"A Havana-seed smoke, gold-leaf adorned, for the people who don't wait for special occasions, but create them."* — set as a single display-italic line at clamp(2.4rem, 5vw, 4rem), centred, on a 28ch measure, no eyebrow, no body copy beneath. A small gold-leaf hairline above the line as a typographic ornament. White ground, generous vertical breathing room.

*Composition.* `<SignatureBlock />`'s lede pattern, deployed without the body paragraph and without the image — a leaner variant. Or: a new minimal `<PullQuote />` component, since the same primitive will earn its keep on other pages later.

*Photography required.* None.

*Reuse.* New micro-component, or `<SignatureBlock />` invoked with empty paragraph + no image. Recommend extracting `<PullQuote />` since it is a small primitive that will be reused.

**6. *The pages where it lives* (pearl ground, two product cross-links).** A compact closing block — two small product cards (Volage, Caprice) sitting side-by-side, each a one-line tagline + a small isolated cigar image + a discover-link. The About page closes by handing the reader to the SKU pages. No "Shop" CTA, no commerce language; the linking is editorial.

*Composition.* Pearl bed (mirrors Section 1's pearl, bookending the page). Two-column at desktop, stacked at mobile. Same isolated-cigar PNG pattern the homepage product slider uses (Option 1 from the product-image research) — cigar on void with a soft drop shadow. Discover link is a single underlined gold-deep word, no button.

*Photography required.* None — existing `cigar-volage.png` and `cigar-caprice.png` from the asset library.

*Reuse.* New small component (`<RangeFootCards />`), or a stripped variant of `<ProductSlider />` set to single-frame mode. Reuse preferred.

**(Footer handoff — global footer reused, no changes.)**

**Section count.** Six.
**Scroll length.** Roughly 4-4.5 viewports at desktop. Long enough to feel substantive, short enough to be read.
**Editorial register.** Founder-quiet, signature-promoted, place-credibled, close on a line.

**Why recommended.** Five reasons.

(a) It uses the *canonical* brand-pager copy as the spine, in the order the brand pager already establishes (flower → signature → founder story → origins → kicker). Stephane has already authorised the brand pager as the brand's own voice; the About page becomes a faithful adaptation rather than a fresh write.
(b) Founder presence is *named and credited* (the section title is *The founder*, the caption names Stephane explicitly) without being staged or biographical. This is the right calibration for a one-founder brand whose founder is the principal — visible enough that distributors know who they are dealing with, restrained enough not to read as personality marketing.
(c) The gold-leaf signature gets its own section *second*, after the flower legend — the same move recommended on /volage. The signature is the brand's only unmistakable visual differentiator and the About page is the second place (after /volage) where promoting it earns out.
(d) Photography burden is low-to-medium: one new founder shot (placeholder pattern if not available v1), one gold-leaf macro (shared with /volage), three origin shots (shared with /volage's eventual upgrade). Nothing required that the /volage roadmap does not already need.
(e) Component reuse is high. `<SignatureBlock />` and `<OriginArc />` already exist; the new primitives (`<PullQuote />`, `<RangeFootCards />`, optional `<OriginProse />`) are small and reusable elsewhere.

### Option B — *The Manifesto* (Le Labo-adjacent, voice-led)

**Description.** A single-page manifesto. The About becomes a *declarative editorial* rather than a biographical or chronological narrative. Inspired by Le Labo's *About Us* but compressed and toned to Mariposa's brand voice (Spectral italic restraint rather than Le Labo's grotesque irreverence). The founder is mentioned once at the close. The signature, the craft, the place all become *statements* rather than passages.

**Section-by-section.**

**1. Header — *A small manifesto* (pearl ground, full-bleed).** ~80vh. Eyebrow `MARIPOSA`. Single Spectral italic display line: *"We don't wait for special occasions. We create them."* (the brand line as the manifesto's headline). No image. No body copy. The line is the whole header.

**2. *We believe* (white ground, centred prose column).** A short numbered list of seven to ten *We believe* statements in Spectral italic at body-display scale (clamp(1.4-1.8rem)), centred, on a 52ch measure. Each statement is one line, no body explanation, generous vertical rhythm between them. Drafted seed list (Axel to polish):
- *We believe a cigar is worth as much patience as the leaf takes.*
- *We believe Havana seed is portable.*
- *We believe twenty years of acclimatisation is not too long to wait for the right flavour.*
- *We believe a cigar should be rolled by hand, by women whose mothers rolled tobacco before them.*
- *We believe a band can be made of gold.*
- *We believe the finer things should not be saved.*
- *We believe a good cigar is an excuse to slow down and start a conversation.*

*Photography required.* None.

**3. *The proof* (white ground, dense prose block).** A single tight prose section pulling the brand pager's Origins paragraphs *as evidence* under the manifesto. Eyebrow `THE PROOF`, then the four brand-pager Origins paragraphs verbatim. This is the credibility payload distributors need; the manifesto above is the voice, this is the substance.

*Photography required.* Optional — one Jember rolling-room shot as a single inline image breaks the prose well.

**4. *The signature* (pearl ground, image-led).** Same as Option A Section 2. Gold-leaf macro paired with the brand-pager signature paragraph.

*Photography required.* Same gold-leaf macro as Option A.

**5. *The maker* (cream ground, single short paragraph + caption).** Founder treatment, compressed. Three short sentences only, lifted from the brand pager Story section: *"Mariposa's story begins with a journey. Driven by a lifelong taste for cigars, founder Stephane Zermatten followed his curiosity to the tobacco fields of East Java. What he discovered inspired him to craft a blend for his own humidor and closest friends."* — one small documentary image to the side. No third or fourth paragraph; the manifesto carries the rest.

*Photography required.* Same founder shot as Option A (or placeholder).

**6. *The pages* (closing).** Same as Option A Section 6 — Volage / Caprice cross-link.

**Section count.** Six.
**Scroll length.** Roughly 3.5-4 viewports.
**Editorial register.** Voice-first, declarative, founder-recessed. The most editorially adventurous of the four.

**Why not recommended (but worth a mock).** Risks: (a) the *We believe* list lives or dies on Axel-drafted copy; an inadequate seed will read as marketing-team voice rather than founder voice, and the section collapses. (b) Manifesto-register works for Le Labo because they have spent two decades calibrating it; Mariposa would be claiming the form before earning it. (c) The founder being mentioned only once and recessed to fifth-section caption-and-paragraph might read as evasive given Stephane *is* the brand at this stage. Strong second pick if Stephane wants the most editorial of the four and is willing to commission an Axel pass on the list.

### Option C — *The single date* (chronology-as-anchor, place-first)

**Description.** A scaled-down chronology, anchored on *one* historical pivot rather than a full timeline. Mariposa does not have 160 years to plot (Plasencia) or 1886 (Glenfiddich), but it does have *1859* — the date Jember's cigar-making heritage begins, per the brand pager. The page opens on that date as the anchoring frame, treats the twenty-year acclimatisation programme as the modern arc, and lets *place* (Jember, Argopuro) carry the brand voice. Macallan / Hennessy register, transplanted.

**Section-by-section.**

**1. Header — *Jember, 1859* (full-bleed photographic ground, landscape).** ~90vh. A single full-bleed photograph of the Jember region (volcanic landscape, tobacco field, or rolling-room exterior). Over it, a single short eyebrow `JEMBER · EAST JAVA · 1859` (Hanken uppercase, gold-leaf, tracked) and a single Spectral italic display line: *"Where Indonesia learned to roll cigars."* (or similar — Axel to polish). The line attaches to the date the way Glenfiddich attaches to 1886. The cigar itself is not in this image; the *place* is.

*Photography required.* **YES.** Full-bleed Jember landscape or BIN factory exterior. Hardest commission of the four options because the shot has to carry the whole header without atmosphere fatigue.

**2. *The land* (pearl ground, two-column).** Eyebrow `THE LAND`. The volcanic-soil / Havana-seed brand-pager paragraph, verbatim: *"Mariposa is hand-rolled in Jember, East Java. A region whose rich cigar-making heritage dates back to 1859. It is also home to Pak Abdul Kahar Muzakir, a cigar aficionado and industry veteran who has dedicated his life to a singular pursuit: capturing the essence of Cuban tradition by cultivating Havana seeds in Indonesian soil."* + *"The wrapper, binder, and filler are all born from Havana-seed tobacco that has spent nearly two decades acclimatising to Jember's volcanic earth. One inline image: Argopuro landscape or tobacco field.

*Photography required.* Argopuro shot (same as Option A).

**3. *The hand* (white ground, two-column).** Eyebrow `THE HAND`. Brand-pager paragraph on rolling: *"Mariposa cigars are entirely hand-rolled by a dedicated team of female rollers, mirroring Cuba's revered torcedoras, many of whom come from families that have worked tobacco for generations. Using long-filler, three-part construction, and entubado bunching for a flawlessly even draw, each cigar is finished with a classic Cuban triple cap. Every single stick is draw-tested and inspected by hand before it leaves the workshop, guaranteeing a flawless smoking experience."*

One inline image: Jember rolling-room (torcedoras at work, hands and leaf — *the* shot of the page).

*Photography required.* Jember rolling-room (same as Option A).

**Section count.** Seven.
**Scroll length.** Roughly 5 viewports — the longest of the four.

### Option D — *The page* (single-screen, type-led, Aesop-adjacent)

**Description.** The radical-restraint option. A single page, type-led, no photographs at all, the whole brand-pager Origins and Story copy set on a quiet ground at body scale with extreme breathing room. The page is the frame; the prose is the page. Aesop / Noma register, applied honestly. The page is shorter, denser per-pixel, and asks the reader to *read* rather than scroll.

**Section-by-section.**

**1. Header — *Mariposa* (white ground, centred column).** ~60vh. Eyebrow `MARIPOSA`. Single display-italic Spectral line: *"For those who don't wait for special occasions, but create them."* (the brand sub from the brand pager, verbatim). Below: small gold-leaf hairline. No image.

**3. *Origins* (white ground, centred prose column).** Continuation. Eyebrow `ORIGINS`. The full brand-pager Origins section verbatim, four paragraphs in body Hanken at body scale, same measure. No subheads. No images interrupting the prose. The unglossed trade terms (escogida, entubado) stay unglossed.

**Section count.** Six (but section breaks are quiet — the page reads as one continuous editorial sheet).
**Scroll length.** Roughly 3 viewports — the shortest.
**Editorial register.** Pure restraint. Most editorially honest, biggest risk on type-only delivery.

**Why not recommended.** Two reasons. (a) The page asks Mariposa to compete with Aesop and Noma on pure type, which is a high bar for a site that has not yet calibrated Spectral at body scale across long-form runs. Will the type hold for 800 words of continuous prose? Yulia should mock and judge before commit. (b) Removing all founder imagery is a *positioning* choice — the page becomes about the brand's voice rather than about the founder, which may or may not be where Stephane wants the About page to land. The brand voice is strong enough to carry the page if the answer is yes; if Stephane wants distributors to know who is behind the brand, this option understates that signal. Strong fallback if v1 photography is unavailable; A is the safer default once photography lands.
